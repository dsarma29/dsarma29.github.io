a little backstory

The best analysts are part detective, part translator, part editor. They find the signal, explain it in plain English, and cut everything that doesn't earn its place. I try to be that.

I started in system integration. REST and SOAP pipelines for Tier-1 banks, ESB integrations across SAP HANA, IBM Maximo, and Ariba. Unglamorous plumbing nobody sees, but it taught me how data actually moves between systems. That foundation is why my analysis tends to hold up under scrutiny.

At Accenture and Software AG I led analytics work for global retailers and banks. I cut reporting defects by 25%, reduced stockouts by 15%, and lifted data integrity by ~20% through root-cause work on POS to inventory mismatches.

Now I'm at USF (3.97 GPA), working remotely from NJ as a Research Assistant on a healthcare ML framework over 7M+ HCUP inpatient records. Open to full-time roles.
